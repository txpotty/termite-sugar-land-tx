Comprehensive Termite Control Solutions

Our termite control service is more than just spray-and-pray. We employ a meticulous, multi-step process to ensure complete eradication of termites and long-term protection for your property. Our detailed approach addresses every aspect of termite management, from initial detection to preventative maintenance.

Initial Inspection and Assessment

The first and most critical step in effective termite control is a thorough inspection. Termites can infest various parts of your home, and recognizing their presence and the extent of the damage requires a trained eye. Our experienced technicians conduct a detailed examination of both the interior and exterior of your property.

What We Look For During Inspection:

  • Mud Tubes: Subterranean termites build these tubes as protected pathways between their underground colonies and your home.
  • Damaged Wood: We inspect for wood that sounds hollow when tapped, has blistering paint, or visible signs of tunneling (galleries) within the wood structure.
  • Discarded Wings: Swarming termites, often mistaken for flying ants, leave behind their wings after finding a new location to establish a colony.
  • Termite Droppings (Frass): Drywood termites leave behind small, pellet-like droppings near infested wood.
  • Signs of Moisture: Areas with excessive moisture can attract termites, so we carefully inspect basements, crawl spaces, and areas around pipes.

Based on the findings of our inspection, we develop a customized treatment plan tailored to the specific type of termite, the severity of the infestation, and the unique construction of your home. This personalized approach ensures the most effective and efficient treatment outcomes.

Targeted Treatment Strategies

We utilize a variety of advanced treatment methods, selecting the most appropriate strategy based on the inspection findings. Our goal is to eliminate the existing termite colony while minimizing disruption to your home and using environmentally responsible products whenever possible.

Common Treatment Methods:

  • Liquid Termiticides: Applied to the soil around the foundation of your home, creating a protective barrier that termites cannot penetrate. Non-repellent termiticides are particularly effective as termites unknowingly travel through the treated soil, carrying the product back to the colony.
  • Termite Baiting Systems: Strategically placed around your property, these systems contain cellulose material laced with a slow-acting termiticide. Termites consume the bait, share it with the colony, and gradually eliminate the population. Baiting systems are an excellent option for ongoing monitoring and prevention.
  • Direct Wood Treatments: For infestations within wooden structures, we may apply termiticides directly into the infested wood. This is often used in conjunction with other treatment methods.
  • Fumigation: In cases of severe or widespread drywood termite infestations that cannot be effectively treated with other methods, fumigation may be necessary. This involves tenting the structure and introducing a gas that eliminates termites throughout the property. (Note: This is a more involved process and is discussed with you in detail if necessary.)

Our technicians are highly trained in the proper application of all treatment methods, ensuring the safety of your family and pets while effectively eradicating termites. We will walk you through the treatment process, explaining what to expect and answering any questions you may have.

Common Termite Problems and How We Help

Recognizing the signs of a termite infestation early is crucial for minimizing damage. Many homeowners miss the initial signs because termites are often hidden within walls or the foundation of the home. Here are some common problems homeowners face with termites and how our professional service provides effective solutions.

Hollow-Sounding Wood

If you tap on wooden structures in your home, such as doorframes, baseboards, or walls, and they sound hollow, it could be an indication that termites are feasting on the interior of the wood. Subterranean termites typically eat the soft cellulose within the wood, leaving a thin veneer. This damage can be significant before it becomes outwardly visible. Our technicians are trained to identify these subtle signs. We use specialized tools, like sounding devices and moisture meters, during our inspection to pinpoint areas of potential termite activity even within walls. Once identified, we can apply targeted treatments to eliminate the termites and assess the extent of the structural damage that may require repair.

Mud Tubes on Walls or Foundation

Subterranean termites build mud tubes, often the width of a pencil, from the ground to your home. These tubes protect them from predators and the dry environment as they travel to find food sources. Finding mud tubes on your exterior walls, foundation, or even inside your garage is a definite sign of a termite infestation. These tubes indicate an active colony is accessing your home. Our service includes a thorough inspection for mud tubes and their entry points. We will follow the tubes to locate the source of the infestation and determine the most effective treatment plan to eliminate the colony and prevent future access through those routes. Removing the tubes themselves is not sufficient; the colony must be addressed at its source.

Piles of Discarded Wings

During certain times of the year, particularly after rain, termites, specifically reproductives called 'swarmers,' emerge from the colony to find a mate and establish new colonies. They are attracted to light and are often seen near windows or doors. After swarming, they shed their wings, leaving behind small piles that resemble fish scales. Finding discarded wings is a strong indicator that a termite colony is present nearby and has potentially established a new satellite colony in your home. This is often one of the first visible signs homeowners notice. Our service will confirm if these are indeed termite wings and conduct a thorough inspection to locate where the swarmers emerged from and if a new infestation has begun. Early intervention at this stage is critical to prevent a widespread infestation.

Damaged Wood or Sagging Floors

Significant termite damage can manifest as visibly damaged wood that appears chewed, blistering or peeling paint on wooden surfaces, or even sagging floors and ceilings due to weakened structural integrity. This level of damage suggests a long-standing and extensive infestation that requires immediate and aggressive treatment. While our primary focus is on eliminating the termites, we can also assess the severity of the structural damage and recommend qualified contractors for repair. Our comprehensive approach ensures that not only are the termites eradicated, but you are also guided through the process of restoring your home's structural soundness and appearance.

Termite Droppings (Frass)

Drywood termites, unlike subterranean termites, do not build mud tubes and live within the wood they infest. A common sign of drywood termites is the presence of frass, which are small, hard, pellet-like droppings that they push out of small holes in the infested wood. The color of the frass can vary depending on the type of wood they are consuming. Finding small piles of what looks like sawdust or sand near wooden furniture or structural elements can indicate a drywood termite issue. Our inspection includes searching for these subtle signs of drywood termites and determining the extent of the infestation within the wood. Treatment for drywood termites often involves localized treatments directly to the infested wood or, in more severe cases, fumigation.
